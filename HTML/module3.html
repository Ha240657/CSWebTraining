Module 3: Device and Network Safety

What is Device and Network Safety?

Device security is the practice of safeguarding devices connected to the internet, e.g. phones, PCs, tablets, etc.

While network security is the protection of the underlying network infrastructure.

These are done to defend against cyber threats and unauthorized access, as well as protection against misuse and theft.

Benefits of Device and Network Security

By safeguarding your devices, it helps protect your devices from threats such as malware, phishing, data breaches as well as theft.

How to Keep Devices Secure

To safeguard your devices:

  • Make sure to lock your screens.
  • Use encryption as well as security apps.
  • Regularly update your device's OS to the latest version.
  • In case the device gets lost, make sure you can remotely wipe any data to protect your personal information.

How to Keep Networks Secure

By having good network security, risks such as cyberthreats and attacks are mitigated, helping protect personal or confidential data by blocking unauthorized access to the network.

To keep your network secure:

  • Use a firewall to monitor incoming and outgoing network traffic.
  • Use VPNs to encrypt Wi-Fi and internet connections.
  • Use strong passwords to block unauthorized access to the network.
